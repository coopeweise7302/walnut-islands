Culture

Language and Religion

The Walnut Islands, while 100% of the population speaks English (fluency in English is a requirement to live in, work at, or even visit the islands, unless one pays a translation fee), we have developed our own "dialect", if one could call it that. These linguistic tendencies include: avoiding slang or youth terms, such as "dank", "fire" and "memes", and also ensuring proper grammar while avoiding words such as "ain't". Furthermore, a large amount of residents speak in a British accent that is clearly fake.


As almost all residents are from the United States upper-class originally, Christianity is the most practiced religion by a large margin. There are fourteen churches, a Protestant and a Catholic church on each island.

Big Game Hunting

As a life of sitting on a desk and ordering around secretaries can get a little dull, we like to spice things up with the exciting yet dangerous sport of big game hunting. Walnut Inc. has gone through great efforts to import exotic animals from Africa. These include: rhinos, lions, elephants, and Cape buffalo. Luckily, no wildlife protection laws exist in the Walnut Islands, so one may hunt freely and without worry of fines.

Food / Food Taboos

While we tend to be very liberal in social customs, we do not enjoy the consumption of low-class foods, such as hot dogs, non-gourmet burgers, corn-fed beef, chocolate without truffles, and frozen food such as T.V. dinners. We are dignified enough to enjoy high quality food products such as lobster, filet mignon, oysters, truffles, and high-end wines and champagnes.

Clothing

As most jobs in the Walnut Islands are in banking or executive management, formal attire (suits, ties, dress shoes) is often worn. All clothing, whether it be casual or formal, is made by a high-end brand, such as Armani or Calvin Klein.
